About Cyberpunk

Cyberpunk is a subgenre of science fiction in a dystopian futuristic setting that tends to focus on a "combination of lowlife and high tech" featuring advanced technological and scientific achievements, such as artificial intelligence and cybernetics, juxtaposed with societal collapse, dystopia or decay.

The genre explores themes of hackers, megacorporations, cybernetics, and artificial intelligence. Cyberpunk plots often center on conflict among artificial intelligences, hackers, and megacorporations, and tend to be set in a near-future Earth, rather than the far-future settings or galactic vistas found in novels.

Cyberpunk Themes

  • The cyberpunk setting is typically a near-future dystopia where advanced technology exists alongside societal breakdown.

  • Large corporations have more influence than governments, leading to corporate dystopias.

  • Human augmentation through cybernetic implants is common, blurring the line between human and machine.

  • Advanced virtual reality and cyberspace environments play a major role in many cyberpunk stories.
